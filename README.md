# Slatepencils-Obsession
Do you like to eat slate pencils too? I did — and that little childhood habit turned into a full market research project.

Growing up, plenty of us secretly nibbled on slate pencils — that chalky, earthy crunch was oddly satisfying. Turns out millions across India share this quiet obsession. But beyond the nostalgia, I had real questions: Who makes these? Which brands dominate? Is eating them safe? Will this industry even survive the next decade?
So I turned a childhood habit into a full market analysis — digging into India's slate pencil industry as a case study in fragmented manufacturing, regional production clusters, brand competition, and a consumer behavior most market reports never touch.

**What's Inside🔍**

**Market share analysis** across 24 brands, scraped and scored from IndiaMART, TradeIndia, and ExportersIndia.

**Production mapping** of India's slate pencil manufacturing hubs (spoiler: it all comes down to one small town in Madhya Pradesh)

**Material science breakdown** — what these things are actually made of.

A deep dive into **usage patterns** — writing vs. the lesser-discussed practice of eating, including real health data and demographic risk analysis.

**Market alternatives & displacement trends** — what's replacing slate pencils, and where they're holding strong.

**An industry outlook**— will this 100-year-old product survive the next decade, or quietly disappear?

**But Why This Project 🤔**
I built this to demonstrate end-to-end project and product thinking: scoping ambiguous research questions, structuring data collection, synthesizing findings into a clear narrative, and packaging it into something a stakeholder could actually use.
