# Slate Pencils Obsession: A Market & Behavioral Analysis of India's Slate Pencil Industry

> *Do you like to eat slate pencils too? I did — and that little childhood habit turned into a full market research project.*

Growing up, plenty of us secretly nibbled on slate pencils — that chalky, earthy crunch was oddly satisfying. Turns out millions across India share this quiet obsession. But beyond the nostalgia, I had real questions: *Who makes these? Which brands dominate? Is eating them safe? Will this industry even survive the next decade?*

So I turned a childhood habit into a full market analysis — digging into India's slate pencil industry as a case study in fragmented manufacturing, regional production clusters, brand competition, and a consumer behavior most market reports never touch.

---

## 🤔 But Why This Project?

I built this to demonstrate end-to-end project and product thinking — scoping an ambiguous research question, structuring data collection from scratch, synthesizing findings into a clear narrative, and packaging it into something a stakeholder could actually use. These are the same skills I bring to project and program management roles every day: turning messy, real-world ambiguity into a structured, decision-ready story.

---

## 🔍 What's Inside

- **Market share analysis** across 24 brands, scraped and scored from IndiaMART, TradeIndia, and ExportersIndia
- **Production mapping** of India's slate pencil manufacturing hubs (spoiler: it mostly comes down to one town in Madhya Pradesh)
- **Material science breakdown** — what these things are actually made of, and how quality varies by tier
- **Usage pattern deep-dive** — writing vs. the lesser-discussed practice of eating, including real health data and demographic risk analysis
- **Market alternatives & displacement trends** — what's replacing slate pencils, and where they're still holding strong
- **Industry outlook** — will this product survive the next decade, or quietly disappear?

---

## 🎯 Key Findings

| Area | Finding |
|---|---|
| **Market Leader** | Chandtara holds 11.12% market presence — the single largest share among 24 brands tracked |
| **Market Concentration** | Top 3 brands (Chandtara, CAT, Tiger) control 29.12% of the market combined |
| **Production Hub** | Mandsaur, Madhya Pradesh accounts for 16 of 24 brands — a major geographic concentration risk |
| **Health Risk** | Heavy, sustained eating-use consumers show a near-universal progression toward severe anemia within a year |
| **Demographic Risk** | Nearly half of all documented pica cases involve pregnant women |
| **Industry Outlook** | Rural/government-school demand stays stable for 5–10 years; urban demand continues to erode; a small international "eating-use" niche is growing online |
